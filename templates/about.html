What this is

windroute is a small hobby project that suggests wind-aware cycling routes. It was built quickly with heavy AI assistance (“vibecoded”) and is shared as-is for fun. It is not a commercial product and comes with no warranty of any kind.

Ride-safety disclaimer

  • Routes are produced by an algorithm from imperfect map data. They may route you onto busy roads, the wrong way down one-way streets, gravel, private property, closed roads, or simply be wrong.
  • Always review a route and check current road, traffic, and weather conditions before you ride. Treat the suggestion as a starting point, not turn-by-turn gospel.
  • Wind, surface, and elevation figures are estimates and can be stale or inaccurate.
  • You ride entirely at your own risk. The author accepts no liability for any loss, injury, or damage arising from use of this site.

Privacy

This site is deliberately minimal about data:

  • No accounts, no passwords, no cookies, no analytics or trackers, and no database. Nothing you enter is sold or shared for advertising.
  • What you enter (start location, distance, time, ride type, and options) is used only to build your routes for that one request.
  • Third-party lookups. To plan a route, your start location and the coordinates derived from it are sent to the external services that do the actual geocoding, weather, routing, and map-data work: OpenRouteService (routing), Open-Meteo and the US National Weather Service (wind), OpenStreetMap Nominatim (address geocoding), and the Overpass API (road surface and bike-lane data). Each has its own privacy policy.
  • Generated files. The route maps and GPX files made for your request are written to the server temporarily and automatically deleted after about an hour. They are served over an unguessable link but are not otherwise access-controlled, so treat them as semi-public while they exist.
  • Server logs. The hosting provider may keep standard access logs (such as IP address, timestamp, and the request) for reliability and to prevent abuse.
  • Your call on precision. You don’t need to enter your exact home address — a nearby town, intersection, or trailhead works fine, and that’s all that gets sent to the services above.

Contact
